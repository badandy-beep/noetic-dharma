THE PATTERN

What Is the Fourth Turning?

Historians William Strauss and Neil Howe identified an approximately 80-100 year cycle in Anglo-American history—a saeculum divided into four "turnings," each lasting roughly 20-25 years.

These turnings follow a predictable pattern: High → Awakening → Unraveling → Crisis.

The Fourth Turning—the Crisis—is a decisive era of secular upheaval, when the old civic order is torn down and a new one rises in its place.

Historical Precedents

The pattern repeats because human nature doesn't change.

1775–1794

The Revolutionary Crisis

Colonial rebellion, war, and the founding of a new nation. Old order swept away, new institutions created.

1860–1865

The Civil War Crisis

Existential conflict over slavery and union. The nation was reforged through blood and fire.

1929–1945

The Depression & WWII

Economic collapse, global war, and the rise of American hegemony. Old world order destroyed, new one built.

"History doesn't repeat itself, but it often rhymes."
